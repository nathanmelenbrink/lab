The drawing below shows the magnetic field around a disc magnet, as seen from the side. Magnetic field as a vector quentity is shown by arrows, representing direction and intensity (arrow length). ![pic](./magfields.jpg) ###Calibration Calibration of the Hall device as a distance sensor. The magnetic field near the axis of the cylindrical magnet points along the axis, and decreases rapidly with distance. The variation of sensor signal with magent distance can be used to make a position sensor. Below is the setup for calibration. Paper cards are added around the sensor to bring the surface up to the level of the sensor face. The magnet is placed in a repeatable way over the sensor, with cardboard spacers used to vary the distance. ![pic](./calib1.jpg) ![pic](./calib2.jpg) ![pic](./calib3.jpg) Below is the calibration data, and a graph on signal vs. distance, in mm. This sensor is quite sensitive for distances less than ~10 mm. As the distance increases from zero, the signal does not go up until about 1mm (not shown in this data.) So the useful range is about 2-8 mm. ![pic](./calib4.jpg) ![pic](./calib5.jpg) ###Accelerometer using Hall sensor.
